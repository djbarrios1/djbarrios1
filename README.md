## How I Think About This Work

Security is mostly reading, configuration, documentation, scripting, and fighting for initiatives that matter. The interesting parts are understanding how systems actually work, finding the gaps between how things are supposed to behave and how they do, and building the capability to know when something has gone wrong before it becomes a catastrophe.

I keep tooling lean on purpose. Every vendor is an attack surface. Every SaaS subscription is data you don't fully control and a configuration you might get wrong. Self-hosting costs time but buys visibility and control that managed services rarely match.

I came to security through real incidents before I had the full theory. That order of operations â€” experience first, then framework â€” means I think about risk in terms of what actually breaks in real environments, not just what the textbook says should.
